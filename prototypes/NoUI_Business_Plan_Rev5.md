Executive Summary

NoUI is a AI-accelerated AI platform that modernizes pension
administration without replacing legacy systems. The platform reads
governing documents, drafts rule configurations for human certification, builds its own
tests, composes task-specific workspaces, and heals itself when laws
change. A coordinated AI ensemble ensures accuracy through multi-model
verification. Absolute data isolation protects every customer while a
shared pattern knowledge base creates a compounding intelligence moat.

The U.S. public pension market encompasses approximately 6,000 state and
local retirement systems managing \$4.5 trillion in assets. Most run on
legacy technology from the 1990s. Replacement projects cost \$20-80M and
fail more than half the time. NoUI deploys in weeks at a fraction of the
cost, with zero disruption to current operations.

This plan reflects a premium pricing model appropriate to the value
delivered. When an agency avoids a \$20-80M replacement project and
receives a modern, AI-accelerated platform deployed in weeks instead of
years, a \$750K+ initial investment represents 1-4% of the cost they
would otherwise incur. The pricing structure consists of three
components: a minimum \$250,000 implementation fee, a \$500,000 initial
product license fee, and an ongoing annual per-member license fee.

The Hidden Cost Argument

The "do nothing" option carries significant hidden costs that justify
NoUI's pricing even for agencies that aren't considering replacement.
These include: staff inefficiency from navigating 15-20 legacy screens
per transaction (estimated at 30-40% productivity loss), calculation
errors resulting in over/underpayments (\$50K-500K annually depending on
plan size), compliance risk from inability to demonstrate rule
traceability, member dissatisfaction from slow processing and opaque
benefit determinations, institutional knowledge loss as experienced
staff retire, and increasing maintenance costs as legacy vendors raise
support fees on aging systems.

For a 30,000-member plan, these hidden costs typically total \$500K-1.5M
annually. NoUI's \$850K Year 1 investment pays for itself within 12
months through operational savings alone, before accounting for the
strategic value of a modern, AI-accelerated platform.

Premium Pricing Levers

Several features unique to NoUI justify premium pricing and
differentiate the offering from any competitor.

1\. Self-Architecting Deployment

Traditional pension system implementations take 2-5 years because every
rule must be manually analyzed, coded, tested, and verified. NoUI's
AI-accelerated system does this in weeks. The agency is paying for
the outcome (a fully configured, tested, deployed system) not the
effort. The fact that AI does most of the work in days rather than
consultants doing it in months is a feature that increases value, not a
reason to reduce price.

2\. Coordinated AI Ensemble Accuracy

NoUI's multi-model verification system catches rule extraction errors
that single-model systems miss. Generate-and-Verify, Adversarial
Challenge, and Parallel Consensus patterns provide multiple independent
checks before any rule reaches production. Every benefit determination
is traceable from the calculated result through the rule definition to
the specific statute section. No existing pension system offers this
level of auditability. This is worth premium pricing because calculation
errors cost agencies real money.

3\. AI-Accelerated Legislative Compliance

When a legislature changes pension law, traditional systems require
months of analysis, coding, and testing to implement the changes. NoUI
detects the change, generates updated rule definitions, regenerates
tests, and presents the changes for human certification --- typically
within hours. The annual per-member fee includes this continuous
legislative compliance. For an agency that currently spends \$100-300K
per year on legislative change management, the annual NoUI fee pays for
itself on this feature alone.

4\. Data Quality Intelligence

The Data Quality Engine finds embedded errors in legacy databases that
the agency didn't know existed. The DERP POC deliberately includes data
quality issues that the system detects: status/date contradictions,
beneficiary allocation errors, contribution balance mismatches. For
agencies that have accumulated decades of data quality problems, this
capability can identify millions of dollars in overpayments and
underpayments.

5\. Progressive Database Migration

Within 12-18 months of deployment, NoUI delivers a clean, modern
PostgreSQL database as a byproduct of normal operation --- without a
traditional data conversion project. A standalone database migration of
comparable scope would cost \$2-5M. This value is included in the
platform at no additional charge.

by positioning implementation + product license (\$750K) as an
operational expenditure under the executive director's authority, not a
capital IT project requiring legislative approval (threshold varies by

professional services engagement with minimal approval requirements
(\$50-150K), creating a foot in the door. If the full engagement exceeds
procurement thresholds, the modular pricing structure allows phased
procurement (implementation first, product license as a separate phase).
